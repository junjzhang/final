My thoughts regarding the Evangelion

I first watched EVA when I was a middle school student. I was shocked since I never wathched any work that is so dark but touch the bottle of my heart. I saw the young love, the first taste of failure, the confusion of young people about the future, and the confrontation between childish self-awareness and the real world. That's what I'm experiencing at that time. This niche and unique work satisfied my need to be different. Going into the obscure settings and deep, dark emotions made me feel like I was spiritually above my peers. Maybe that's how I fell in love with this work while being pretentious and empathetic.

I grew up and more people around me came to see this work. I stopped using it as a capital to show off and started to understand this work from other perspectives. I learned about its influence on the Japanese animation industry, its artwork, its soundtrack, and its behind-the-scenes anecdotes. A new series was launched, and it seemed much more positive. Last year, the last one was finally released, and I couldn't help but cry when I saw the people in the show reconciled with themselves and returned to real life.

This is my favorite scene from the Final. After all the treacherous battles and inner torment, shinji is back to real life with Mari.

They are reconciled to themselves and are completed.

Sure, the otaku has grown up, middle-aged boys have to enter society. Goodbye, my youth. Goodbye, all the Evangelions.
